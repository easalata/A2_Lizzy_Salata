Subtheme: Exploring Speculative Investment ("Flipped Properties")

    This dataset was used to create MAPC's report "Homes for Profit" in 2023. It contains residential sales data strictly for the city of Boston from 2000 to 2023. The data encompasses 63 metrics including categorizations such as type of property, zipcode, buying/ selling price, buying/ selling entity, and much more. I chose to analyze this particular dataset because I feel as though it was the most relevant to my own experiences. For example, when I was living in Reston, VA prior to MIT, the apartment building I was living in changed management and I experienced some of the degredations in customer service and living ammenities as discussed in the readingings (2). Additionally, the triplex located next to my current apartment was recently bought, flipped, and now is back on the market.

Overall Analysis Questions

  1. How does overall tennant satisfaction change when landlords switch to private equity firms?
  2. What percentage of properties are purchased with cash?
  3. How does the difference in price between house flips change relative to what type of entity is purchasing and selling the property (i.e. bought and sold through speculation or not)?
  4. Which neighborhoods in the Greater Boston Area are being most affected by flipped properties?
  5. What types of entities are buying and selling these properties? (i.e. banks, individuals, LLC, etc.)
  6. Is there any data missing from this dataset that should be included in future studies to mitigate bias?

Discoveries & Insights

    There is much to discover about this dataset, and an appropriate starting position for this project is to try and understand the scale of transactions, what types of properties are being bought and sold and at what frequency, and how many properties are being 'flipped.'
